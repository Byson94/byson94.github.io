---
title: Websites of Programming Languages
date: 2026-06-26
description: The website of all programming languages suck!
---

I recently have been exploring some programming a bit and noticed one thing in common. All of their websites 
have terrible UI. Something straight out of the 2000s. 

Don't believe me? Take a look at both go's and rust's websites:

- https://go.dev
- https://rust-lang.org

They are extremely minimal and looks classy. I'm not hating on their look, I myself appreciate websites 
that have classic UI's because they load fast and does not have information scrattered all around like modern websites.
But how can the websites of all programming languages coincidentally have the same classic UI style?

I believe the answer to this question lies in the same paragraph above. I mentioned that I appreciate websites
that have classic UI's because they load fast and are quite text dense. And maybe that **is the point**.
The languages are not trying to be flashy, they are targetting the actual audience who consumes the product.

But that raises another question. What if the websites weren't like so? Would developers still use the website?
Or would it mark a new standard of first impression to developers? As much curious I am to know the answer,
it is up to the developers of the respective language to experiment with this idea. And I don't think that will 
be happening anytime soon.
